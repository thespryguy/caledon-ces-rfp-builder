Caledon CES RFP Workspace — Partner How-To Guide

Plain-English operating guide for HCE, MIES, Mantle, and bid/compliance contributors.

What this tool is for

The workspace is an internal collection and drafting tool for the Caledon Community Energy Systems Study proposal. It helps each partner add their assigned inputs, track missing items, stage portal forms, run basic QA checks, and submit updates to the bid coordinator.

Important: This workspace is not the Town submission portal. Final submission still happens through the official Bids&Tenders portal after the bid manager confirms addenda, pricing, references, legal entity details, and upload requirements.

Partner workflow in 7 steps

1

Open the app and select your role

Start on Dashboard. Click your role card: HCE, MIES, Mantle, Bid/Commercial/Compliance, or Show All. This filters the workspace to your lane.

2

Go to My Focus

My Focus shows your required inputs, missing items, next actions, and proposal sections that need your input.

3

Complete Partner Workspaces first

Use Partner Workspaces to fill assigned fields. These are the safest place to add partner content, assumptions, resumes, references, rates, conflict notes, and scope details.

4

Review the Five Upload Sections

Use Five Upload Sections for proposal-facing wording only. Keep this clean, evaluator-friendly, and free of internal notes where possible.

No pricing in Sections 1–5: Do not enter fees, hourly rates, workshare values, or task pricing into the technical upload sections.
5

Stage portal form data

Use Portal Forms for bid submission fields, references, addenda, pricing schedule placeholders, hourly rates, and subcontractor records. Treat this as staging only; final upload still requires portal verification.

6

Run QA / Readiness

Use QA / Readiness before submitting your lane. Look for incomplete references, pricing leakage, missing hourly rates, subcontractor cap risk, and unconfirmed partner claims.

7

Submit and export a backup

Use Submit to Coordinator when your lane is ready. Then immediately use Export JSON Backup so the bid manager has a fallback if the Google submission fails.

Quality rules for everyone

Do
  • Use clear, factual language.
  • Label assumptions and gaps.
  • Mark complete items Ready for QA.
  • Export JSON backup after meaningful edits.
  • Keep HCE as prime/final integrator in the narrative.
Do not
  • Put pricing in Sections 1–5.
  • Present unconfirmed partners as confirmed.
  • Invent staff names, hours, rates, or references.
  • Contact the Town outside the approved procurement process.
  • Overwrite another partner’s lane without coordination.

Coordinator checklist

  1. Confirm all partners have submitted their lane or JSON backup.
  2. Import/review partner submissions.
  3. Resolve critical blockers: legal entity, references, rates, workshare, staff, conflicts, addenda.
  4. Check subcontractors remain below 50% of total contract price.
  5. Run no-pricing-in-technical check.
  6. Export Sections 1–5 and review formatting.
  7. Confirm final portal fields against the live Bids&Tenders page and addenda.
  8. Save a final JSON backup before upload.

Troubleshooting

The app looks stale or wrong

Hard refresh the browser. If still wrong, export a JSON backup, then use Backup / Import only if the bid manager confirms reset is safe.

Submit to Coordinator fails

Your local save still exists. Export JSON Backup and send that file to the bid coordinator.

I cannot see my fields

Go to Dashboard and confirm the correct role is selected. Use Show All only if you need the full workspace.

I am not sure where to enter something

Use Partner Workspaces for draft input and flag it in Notes to bid manager when submitting.
